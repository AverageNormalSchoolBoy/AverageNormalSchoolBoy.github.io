Research Overview

My work focuses on order-theoretic and categorical aspects of lattices, with applications to algebraic geometry and model theory.

Specifically, I study the correspondence between algebraic structures and the topological spaces they induce. For instance, the lattice of ideals of a ring can be analyzed using morphisms between distributive lattices, which can be represented diagrammatically.

Example: Morphisms of a Finite Lattice

Below is a simple quiver representing a morphism between lattices $L$ and $M$. 

\begin{tikzpicture}[->, >=stealth, node distance=1.5cm, auto]
  \node (L1) at (0,0) {$0_L$};
  \node (L2) at (0,1.5) {$a$};
  \node (L3) at (0,3) {$1_L$};

  \node (M1) at (3,0) {$0_M$};
  \node (M2) at (3,1.5) {$b$};
  \node (M3) at (3,3) {$1_M$};

  \draw (L1) -- (L2) -- (L3);
  \draw (M1) -- (M2) -- (M3);
  \draw[dashed] (L1) -- (M1);
  \draw[dashed] (L2) -- (M2);
  \draw[dashed] (L3) -- (M3);
\end{tikzpicture}

This simple diagram corresponds to a lattice homomorphism preserving joins and meets, indicated by the dashed arrows. TikZJax renders this diagram dynamically in the browser.

Another Example: A Commutative Square

\begin{tikzpicture}[node distance=2cm, auto]
  \node (A) {$A$};
  \node (B) [right of=A] {$B$};
  \node (C) [below of=A] {$C$};
  \node (D) [below of=B] {$D$};

  \draw[->] (A) to node[above] {$f$} (B);
  \draw[->] (A) to node[left] {$g$} (C);
  \draw[->] (B) to node[right] {$h$} (D);
  \draw[->] (C) to node[below] {$k$} (D);
\end{tikzpicture}

In categorical terms, this diagram commutes if $h \circ f = k \circ g$. You can mix LaTeX math and TikZ freely within your pages.

Long-Term Directions

I'm currently exploring how certain lattice embeddings can be characterized via functors on the category of finite posets, with attention to how limits and colimits behave under these embeddings.

Mathematically, this means analyzing constructions of the form $$ \varprojlim_{i \in I} L_i, \quad \varinjlim_{j \in J} M_j $$ and understanding how they interact under morphisms preserving joins and meets.
